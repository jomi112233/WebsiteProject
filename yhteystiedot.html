About Us

In 1890, at the crossroads of Finland’s changing cultural landscape, a small yet ambitious French restaurant named Le Boeuf Chaud opened its doors in Helsinki. Founded by Henri Dubois, a young French chef with a dream to bring the heart of Paris to the northernmost corners of Europe, the restaurant was a symbol of the growing interest in international cuisine during a time when Helsinki was still under Russian rule.
